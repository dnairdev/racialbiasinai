ALGORITHMIC BIAS IN RECRUITING

Home

Companies are turning to artificial intelligence-driven tools to recruit and hire employees. We may think that AI is the key to eradicate human bias and end all forms of discrimination in the hiring process. However, AI hiring tools are trained by humans using datasets from the real world, so they are feeded with all the preexisting human biases when making hiring decisions.

The Al hiring tools will most likely reflect the characteristics of the current workforce and decisions made by past employers. A company with good intentions can not ensure a neutral set of data within the Al hiring tools. Companies are hoping that algorithms can add consistency to the hiring process, but these algorithms can replicate and amplify institutional and historical biases.

On the surface, the Al hiring tools may seem like an appealing alternative to biased human evaluation and decisions but the sets of data inside these tools encode subjective decisions and judgements.

To consider where the data used to develop the Al hiring systems comes from: The data can be enhanced and diversified by acquiring multiple external sets of data.

Input of different sets of data for the AI hiring system, instead of designing the program to look for candidates depending on school name, quality resume rather direct it towards a specific skill or required education and experience.

To recognize that AI bias exists and constantly monitor for bias. Finally, to understand how different training data affect their behaviour and hiring outcome
